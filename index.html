About the project

Scattered results, brought into one research-ready collection

Bubble plume data appear across journal figures, tables, appendices, and supplementary files. Differences in terminology, units, and reporting make studies difficult to compare.

This repository will preserve each dataset alongside its publication, experimental conditions, measurement method, and interpretation notes. The aim is a resource that researchers and students can inspect, download, cite, and build upon with confidence.

Data model

Context travels with every dataset

Each entry is designed to answer three practical questions: Where did the data come from? What does each value mean? What should a researcher know before reusing it?

01

Source publication

Citation, authors, publication year, study purpose, and a concise summary of the experiment.

02

Structured dataset

CSV files with descriptive column names, units, variable definitions, and source figure or table.

03

Reusable metadata

Plume regime, diffuser type, gas flow rate, measurement method, and dimensional basis.

Source paper and datasets

Li et al. 2020 — Integral characteristics of bubble plumes

Eight nondimensionalized datasets for air-stone and single-orifice diffuser cases, covering plume half-width, centerline velocity, volume flux, and momentum flux.
