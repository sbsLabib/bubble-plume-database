Paper synopsis

Li et al. (2020) experimentally investigated how bubble size and bubble population modify the integral behavior of bubble plumes in quiescent, unstratified water. The study compares air-stone diffuser cases with many small bubbles against single-orifice diffuser cases with fewer larger bubbles. Using PIV-based velocity measurements, the paper reports the evolution of plume half-width, centerline velocity, liquid volume flux, momentum flux, entrainment coefficient, and momentum amplification factor, and evaluates nondimensional scaling using the plume length scale D and bubble slip velocity W_s.

Data notes

Processing and metadata status

  • The cleaned CSV files contain nondimensionalized plotting data.
  • Rows were sorted numerically by z_hat.
  • Air-stone and single-orifice datasets are stored separately.
  • Q1/Q2 or curve-level labels are not yet included unless explicitly available in the CSV files.
  • Future metadata may add case-level labels, gas flow rate, diffuser type, and bubble-size information.
